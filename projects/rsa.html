RSA:For the Long Time

OurStory

How might we encourage people and communities to think and act for the long term?

Problem: From researching the diversity in the UK, a startling statistic showed that “4 out of 10 Britons agreed that multicultural Britain has ‘undermined British culture’”. Britain struggles with its diversity, especially in schools. With such a euro-centric look on history and many other subjects, the minority groups feel unrepresented.

Process: Whistle blowing teachers spoke out about the impact of ‘narrow education’ as well as a history curriculum too devoted to ‘the Tudors and Hitler’. To gather first hand feedback, I spoke with my secondary school history teacher who stated that “the problem is that the education system moves too slowly” and also agrees that there is a focus on British history. History needs modernising and expanding to cater for a multicultural Britain.

Proposal: Our story is an intervention that will allow children to choose a topic of history that interests them most. changing the Eurocentric views of traditional history whilst simultaneously modernising it in a blended learning approach we can begin to change some of the racial stigmas that are associated with certain ethnicities. Knowledge is integral to removing a close-minded attitude towards different cultures.
